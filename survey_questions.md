## Likert type question: What are the barriers you face when working with pathogen data?

Please consider the following definitions:

- Significant Barrier: A frequent or major issue that significantly limits your ability to make the best possible use of pathogen data.
- Not a Significant Barrier: An infrequent or minor issue that does not substantially impact your ability to work with pathogen data.
- Not Applicable: This barrier does not apply to your use of pathogen data.

### Possible barriers

- Restricted Access to Data:(e.g., paywalls, restricted databases, or lengthy and complex approval processes that limit timely access to pathogen data).
- Unclear Data-Sharing Policies:(e.g., ambiguity around what data can be shared, when it can be shared, and with whom, creating uncertainty and hesitation in data-sharing practices).
- Data Security Challenges:(e.g., limited access to secure infrastructure or tools required for handling sensitive pathogen data safely and in compliance with regulations).
- Data Aggregation Challenges:(e.g., pathogen data that is siloed across systems, organizations, or formats, making it difficult to combine and share even when no explicit policy prohibits it).
- Insufficient Technical Resources: (e.g., lack of access to high-performance computing systems or software, unreliable internet connectivity, or limited server capacity for managing pathogen data).
- Shortage of Skilled Personnel: (e.g., an insufficient number of data scientists, bioinformaticians, or other experts either within the team or at organizational level).
- Barriers to Training, Education, and Capacity Building: (e.g., limited availability of training programs or workshops, higher-education degree programs, or workforce development programs ).
- Limited Funding for Work: (e.g., insufficient budget for conducting experiments, acquiring computational tools, or accessing subscription journals and services).
- Legal Barriers or Uncertainty:(e.g., restrictions or constraints imposed by data protection laws such as GDPR or HIPAA, which limit access, sharing, or use of pathogen data, including ambiguity in data sharing and compliance).
- Potential for Data Misuse: (e.g., concerns about unauthorized use, misinterpretation of shared data, or issues related to data sovereignty and ownership).
- Intellectual Property Restrictions: (e.g., data ownership claims or proprietary rights preventing open access or collaboration on pathogen datasets).
- Informed Consent Limitations:(e.g., difficulty obtaining or verifying consent for the use of clinical or personal data in research, particularly in international datasets).
- Inadequate Recognition for Data Contributions:(e.g., lack of citation, acknowledgment, or credit for researchers or institutions that contribute pathogen data to studies or publications).
- Trust Issues Between Stakeholders: (e.g., reluctance to share data due to fears of unfair competition, exploitation, or lack of equitable benefit-sharing among contributors).
- Difficulty in Translating Data Insights into Policy: (e.g., challenges in ensuring that insights derived from pathogen data reach policymakers or government organizations capable of turning them into actionable policies).
- Lack of Interdisciplinary Collaboration: (e.g., limited communication or cooperation between disciplines such as epidemiology, microbiology, genetics, and policymaking, which hinders holistic decision-making and solutions).
- Social and Public Perceptions:(e.g., challenges in communicating the rationale for your work, including clearly explaining its benefits and potential risks)
